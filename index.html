The timeline of Elon Musk's life:

  • 1971 - Elon Musk is born in Pretoria, South Africa, and was baptized into the Anglican church. His father, Errol Musk, is a South African electromechanical engineer, pilot, sailor, consultant, and property developer.
  • 1989 - Musk arrived in Cana in June and lived with a second cousin in Saskatchewan for a year, working odd jobs at a farm and lumber-mill.
  • 1999 - Musk co-founded X.com, on online financial service and e-mail payment company.
  • 2000 - X.com merged with online back Confinity to avoid competition, as Confinity's money transfer service PayPal was more popular than X.com's service.
  • 2001 - Musk became involved with the nonprofit Mars Society and discussed funding plans to place growth-chamber for plants on Mars.
  • 2002 - Musk founded SpaceX in May 2002 and became the company's CEO and Chief Engineer.
  • 2004 - Musk led the Series A round of investment. He invested $6.5 million, became the majority shareholder, and joinded Tesla's board of directors as chairman.
  • 2006 - SpaceX attempted its first launch of the Falcon 1 rocket. Though the rocket failed to reach Earth orbit, it was awarded a Commercial Orbital Transportation Services program contract from NASA Administrator (and former SpaceX consultant) Mike Griffin later that year.
  • 2008 - Musk assumed leadership of Tesla as CEO and product architect. Tesla first build an electric sport car, the Roadster, with sales of about 2,500 vehicles.
  • 2012 - Tesla began delivery of its four-door Model S sedan.
  • 2013 - Musk announced plans for a version of a vactrain-a vacuum tube train- and assigned a dozen engineers from SpaceX and Tesla to establish the conceptual foundations and create intial designs.
  • 2014 - Musk promoted the idea of SolarCity building and advanced production facility in Buffalo, New Yor, triple the size of the largest solar plant in the United States.
  • 2016 - Musk co-founded Neuralink, a neurotechnology startup company, with an investment of $100 million.
  • 2017 - Musk founded the Boring Company to construct tunnels, and revealed plans for specialized, underground, high-occupancy vehicles that could travel up to 150 miles per hour (240 km/h) and thus circumvent above-ground traffic in major cities.
  • 2019 - The Cybertruck, an all-electric pickup truck, was unveiled.
  • 2020 - Tesla launched the Mode Y crossover.
  • 2021 - The Boring Company completed a tunnel beneath the Las Vegas Convention Center.
  • 2022 - After buying up enough stock to make him a majority shareholder by April 1, Musk made the move to purchase Twitter for $44 billion at the end of April. His plans include making Twitter “better than ever” by “enhancing the product with new features.
